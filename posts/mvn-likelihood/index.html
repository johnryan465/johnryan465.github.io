In this post we will be looking at the idea of attempting to calculate the expected value of the product of non independent normal distributions.

E[∏i=1nXi],[X1⋮Xn]∼N(μ,Σ) E\left[\prod_{i=1}^n X_i\right], \begin{bmatrix}X_1 \\ \vdots \\ X_n\end{bmatrix} \sim N \left(\mu , \Sigma \right)E[∏i=1n​Xi​],⎣⎢⎢⎡​X1​⋮Xn​​⎦⎥⎥⎤​∼N(μ,Σ)

The simplest thing to do is kinda a cop-out, if we implicitly assume independence we get the following result.

E[∏i=1nXi]=∏i=1nE[Xi]=∏i=1nμiE\left[\prod_{i=1}^n X_i\right] = \prod_{i=1}^nE\left[ X_i\right] = \prod_{i=1}^n \mu_i E[∏i=1n​Xi​]=∏i=1n​E[Xi​]=∏i=1n​μi​

This can be useful as it is very simple to compute, but this assumption isn't always valid.

Calculating the intergral for the expectation directly analytically is difficult.

We will use the following theorem:

(From wikipedia)

Isserlis' Theorem

If (X1,…,Xn)\left(X_1,\ldots,X_n\right)(X1​,…,Xn​) is a zero-mean multivariate normal vector, then

E[X1…Xn]=∑p∈Pn2∏{i,j}∈PE[Xi,Xj]=∑p∈Pn2∏{i,j}∈PCov(Xi,Xj)E\left[X_1\ldots X_n \right] = \sum_{p \in P^2_n} \prod_{\{i,j\} \in P} E\left[X_i,X_j\right] = \sum_{p \in P^2_n} \prod_{\{i,j\} \in P} Cov\left(X_i,X_j\right)E[X1​…Xn​]=∑p∈Pn2​​∏{i,j}∈P​E[Xi​,Xj​]=∑p∈Pn2​​∏{i,j}∈P​Cov(Xi​,Xj​)

where the sum is over all the pairings of (1,…,n)\left( 1, \ldots, n\right)(1,…,n)

Note: This implies that if n∈2N+1n \in 2\mathbf{N} + 1n∈2N+1 , as we will have no valid pairings that our expectation will be 0.

This is close to what we want, but we unfortunely don't have a 0 mean normal vectors, we have arbitary finite means.
